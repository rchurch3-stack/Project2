Plot 1: Bar Chart

This bar chart shows the number of customers who did and did not subscribe to the term deposit.

ggplot(bank, aes(x = y)) +
  geom_bar() +
  labs(
    title = "Term Deposit Subscription Counts",
    x = "Subscribed to Term Deposit",
    y = "Number of Customers"
  )

Most customers did not subscribe to the term deposit. Only a small portion of customers chose to subscribe.

Plot 2: Side-by-side bar chart

This bar chart compares customers with and without a housing loan based on whether they subscribed to the term deposit.

ggplot(bank, aes(x = housing, fill = y)) +
  geom_bar(position = "dodge") +
  labs(
    title = "Subscription Status by Housing Loan",
    x = "Has Housing Loan",
    y = "Number of Customers",
    fill = "Subscribed"
  )

Most customers did not subscribe, regardless of housing loan status. Customers without a housing loan appear slightly more likely to subscribe than those with a housing loan.

Plot 3: Histogram

This faceted histogram shows the distribution of customer ages by subscription status, with separate panels for each marital status.

ggplot(bank, aes(x = age, fill = y)) +
  geom_histogram(binwidth = 5, color = "white") +
  facet_wrap(~ marital) +
  labs(
    title = "Age Distribution by Subscription Status and Marital Status",
    x = "Age",
    y = "Number of Customers",
    fill = "Subscribed"
  )

The age distribution is similar across marital status groups, and in every group more customers did not subscribe than those who did.

Plot 4: Scatter plot

This scatter plot shows the relationship between a customer’s age and account balance. The points are colored by whether the customer subscribed to the term deposit.

ggplot(bank, aes(x = age, y = balance, color = y)) +
  geom_point(alpha = 0.4) +
  labs(
    title = "Age vs. Account Balance by Subscription Status",
    x = "Age",
    y = "Account Balance",
    color = "Subscribed"
  )

There is no strong relationship between age and account balance. Most customers have relatively low balances, regardless of whether they subscribed, although a few customers have much higher balances.

Plot 5: Box Plot

This box plot compares the distribution of account balances for customers who did and did not subscribe to a term deposit. It helps identify differences in the median, spread, and potential outliers between the two groups.

ggplot(bank, aes(x = y, y = balance, fill = y)) +
  geom_boxplot() +
  labs(
    title = "Account Balance by Subscription Status",
    x = "Subscribed to Term Deposit",
    y = "Account Balance",
    fill = "Subscribed"
  )

The box plot shows that customers who subscribed (“yes”) generally have a slightly higher account balance than those who did not subscribe (“no”). Both groups contain many outliers, indicating that a small number of customers have unusually large account balances.
